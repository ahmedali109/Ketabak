Our Authors

Ernest Hemingway

American novelist and Nobel Prize winner known for his distinctive writing style. His works include "The Old Man and the Sea", "A Farewell to Arms", and "For Whom the Bell Tolls". His minimalist prose revolutionized modern literature.

7 Novels Nobel Prize 1954

George Orwell

English novelist and essayist famous for his dystopian masterpieces "1984" and "Animal Farm". His works explore themes of totalitarianism, social injustice, and the dangers of political manipulation. A powerful voice of the 20th century.

6 Novels Numerous Essays

F. Scott Fitzgerald

Iconic American author of the Jazz Age, best known for "The Great Gatsby". His novels capture the glamour and disillusionment of 1920s America. Considered one of the greatest American writers of the 20th century.

4 Novels 164 Short Stories

J.R.R. Tolkien

British author and philologist who created Middle-earth. His epic fantasy works "The Hobbit" and "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy redefined the fantasy genre and inspired countless writers and filmmakers worldwide.

25+ Books Father of Modern Fantasy

Stephen King

Master of horror and suspense with over 60 novels to his name. His iconic works include "The Shining", "It", and "The Stand". Known as the King of Horror, his stories blend supernatural elements with deep human emotions.

60+ Novels 200+ Short Stories

William Shakespeare

The greatest playwright in English literature. His timeless works include "Romeo and Juliet", "Hamlet", and "Macbeth". His profound understanding of human nature continues to resonate with audiences 400 years later.

39 Plays 154 Sonnets
